Welcome

Human beings are social creatures. Society is formed by individuals. Out of a desire to lead a disciplined life built on bonds of affection, respect, and compassion, people form communities. Through this, they experience immense joy and peace. Across the world, people enjoy social life within distinct national identities, differentiated by time, place, and circumstances. It is through these bonds that various castes and communities have emerged.

The Pandara community is a transformation of agricultural people. They have unique expertise and transparency in producing various types of vegetable crops in gardens and orchards. Earning a livelihood through vegetable farming is a hallmark of the Pandara community. Their role in supplying various vegetables to all sections of society is extremely significant.

The Pandara community is essentially a collective of vegetable-farming families. They have maintained their distinct identity since ancient times by adopting vegetable cultivation in gardens and fields as their profession. In local language, this community is called "Padaria," which over time evolved into "Pandara."

The Government of India has registered the Pandara community as Other Backward Classes (OBC) at serial number 126, and the Government of Odisha has registered it as Socially and Educationally Backward Classes (SEBC) at serial number 130.

In 1958, some well-wishers of the Pandara Samaja organized a meeting at Bhetanai in Ganjam district, bringing together all community members to establish and declare their collective identity.
