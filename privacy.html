Privacy Policy

In plain English: This website is a private community platform for the Pandara community of Odisha. We collect only the information needed to run the member directory, matrimony section, family tree, and event/announcement features. We do not sell your data. Matrimony and member data is visible only to signed-in members, never to the public. You can ask us to see, correct, or delete your data at any time.

1. Who we are

This Privacy Policy applies to Nikhila Odisha Pandara Samaja (“we”, “our”, “the Samaja”) and the website and portal at nikhilaodishapandarasamaja.in, including the Members Portal, Admin Portal, and Matrimony section (together, the “Services”).

For the purposes of the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (“DPDP Act”), Nikhila Odisha Pandara Samaja is the Data Fiduciary. You, as a user of the Services, are the Data Principal.

2. What personal data we collect

2.1 Data you provide directly

  • Account data: name, username, password (stored hashed, never in plain text), phone number, email address.
  • Member directory data: district, taluka, panchayat, village, date of birth, gender, occupation, marital status, family head name.
  • Matrimony data (sensitive): profile photo, height, complexion, education, occupation, income range, expected partner preferences, and anything else you voluntarily enter in a matrimony profile.
  • Family tree data: relationships between members, family album photos, family event details.
  • User-generated content: blog posts, feed posts, comments, reactions, reviews, event RSVPs.

2.2 Data collected automatically

  • Technical data: IP address, browser type, device type, operating system, referrer, and pages visited.
  • Session data: authentication tokens, login timestamps, multi-factor-authentication (MFA) status.
  • Analytics: aggregated usage statistics via Google Analytics (anonymised IP where supported).

2.3 What we do not collect

  • We do not collect Aadhaar, PAN, bank account, or credit-card numbers.
  • We do not collect biometric data (fingerprints, facial recognition vectors).
  • We do not knowingly collect data from children under 18. If we learn we have, we will delete it. Parents or guardians can write to our Grievance Officer.

3. Why we process your data (purpose & legal basis)

Under section 4 of the DPDP Act, we process your personal data on the following lawful bases:

  • Your consent (given when you register, when you create a matrimony profile, and when you upload photos).
  • Legitimate uses — specifically, responding to your requests, maintaining community records, and protecting the Samaja from fraud or abuse.

We use your data to:

  • Provide the Members Portal, Matrimony section, Family Tree, and Blog features.
  • Authenticate you, detect brute-force attacks, and enforce rate limits.
  • Send community announcements, meeting notices, and notifications you have opted into.
  • Moderate user-generated content (posts, comments, matrimony profiles) and remove anything unlawful, false, or defamatory.
  • Comply with lawful government requests under Indian law.
  • Understand usage in aggregate so we can improve the Services.

We do not use your data for advertising targeting, and we do not sell or rent your personal data to any third party.

4. Who can see your data

4.1 Other members

The member directory and matrimony section are visible only to signed-in, verified members. They are not visible to the public internet or to search engines. Your blog posts and feed posts are visible to other members unless marked private.

4.2 Administrators

A small number of elected or appointed admins can view the full directory and matrimony data for moderation purposes. All admin accounts require multi-factor authentication (MFA). Admin actions are logged in an audit trail.

4.3 Third-party processors

We use the following processors, who handle your data only on our instructions:

  • Google LLC (Firebase Storage, Firebase Authentication, Analytics) — file storage, authentication infrastructure, anonymised analytics. Data may be stored on Google servers outside India.
  • Render (Render Services, Inc.) — hosts our backend API.
  • Cloudflare, Inc. — provides CAPTCHA (Turnstile) to stop automated abuse.
  • GitHub Pages (GitHub, Inc.) — hosts our public website.

Some of these processors are located outside India. We transfer your personal data to them only under the conditions permitted by section 16 of the DPDP Act, and only to countries not restricted by the Central Government.

5. How long we keep your data (retention)

  • Active accounts: retained for as long as your account is active.
  • Matrimony profiles: deleted within 30 days of the earliest of (a) your request, (b) a marriage match you confirm, or (c) 24 months of inactivity.
  • Deleted accounts: personal data is removed within 30 days, except where retention is required by law.
  • Audit logs: retained for 12 months for security purposes, then deleted.
  • Backups: may contain your data for up to 90 days after deletion, after which they are overwritten.

6. Your rights as a Data Principal

Under the DPDP Act, 2023, you have the following rights. To exercise any of them, contact our Grievance Officer (see section 10).

  • Right to access: receive a summary of the personal data we hold about you, the purposes of processing, and the third parties with whom it has been shared.
  • Right to correction & erasure: have inaccurate data corrected or request deletion of your data.
  • Right to grievance redressal: raise a complaint with our Grievance Officer. If unresolved within 15 days, you may approach the Data Protection Board of India.
  • Right to nominate: nominate another person to exercise your rights in the event of your death or incapacity.
  • Right to withdraw consent: at any time, with the same ease as giving it. Withdrawing consent does not affect lawful processing that happened before withdrawal.

7. How we secure your data

  • All traffic uses HTTPS with TLS 1.2 or higher.
  • Passwords are stored using industry-standard one-way hashing (bcrypt or argon2). We cannot recover your password; only reset it.
  • Admin accounts require MFA (TOTP).
  • Login forms are protected by Cloudflare Turnstile CAPTCHA and server-side rate limiting.
  • File uploads are restricted by content-type and size, and stored in an access-controlled Firebase Storage bucket.
  • We run automated dependency-vulnerability scans and patch known issues.

No system is 100% secure. In the event of a personal-data breach that is likely to cause harm, we will notify affected Data Principals and the Data Protection Board of India as required by the DPDP Act.

8. Cookies

We use two kinds of cookies and similar technologies:

  • Strictly necessary: authentication tokens, CSRF tokens, session state. Without these the Portal cannot function.
  • Analytics: Google Analytics (only loaded if you accept via the cookie banner).

You can refuse analytics cookies in the banner or in your browser settings without losing access to the Portal.

9. Children

The Services are intended for adults (18+). In line with the DPDP Act, if we become aware that we hold the personal data of a child without verifiable parental consent, we will delete that data. We do not use the data of children or persons with disabilities for tracking, behavioural monitoring, or targeted advertising.

We may update this Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will post a notice on the homepage and, where possible, send an email or in-app notification to registered members. The “Last updated” date at the top of this page always reflects the latest version.

12. Governing law

This Privacy Policy is governed by the laws of India, including the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, the Information Technology Act, 2000, and the rules made thereunder. Any dispute is subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Odisha.
